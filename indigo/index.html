Section 1.5

What Even Is Paneer?

Paneer is a mild, firm, fresh Indian cottage cheese that does not melt in these dishes. Think halloumi but gentler — kind of bouncy and gummy and delicious.

It tends to absorb whatever sauce it is in, which makes it a very efficient flavor delivery cube.

You already love it — kids have been asking for it and eating it for years.

Section 4

What To Order At Indigo Martha’s

These are designed as low-drama entry points. Order a few, share everything, and let the table build confidence through repetition.

For Mom

Vegetarian, open to some heat.

  • Saag Paneer — your kid’s favorite. Creamy spinach, firm cheese. A safe landmark.
  • Paneer Butter Masala — the gentle, crowd-pleasing gateway dish.
  • Malai Kofta — veggie dumplings in a silky cream sauce. Wildcard. Worth it.

For Dad

Meat eater, new to this.

  • Butter Chicken (OG) — the international ambassador of Indian food. Creamy, mild, perfect entry point.
  • Chicken Tikka Masala — slightly bolder than butter chicken; bell peppers add familiarity.
  • Tandoori Chicken — straight off the clay oven, recognizable as “just good grilled chicken.”

Breads + Starter

  • Plain or Garlic Naan — use it to scoop. No utensils required, but they are available, Dad.
  • Samosa — fried, potato-filled pastry. The Indian knish. Very approachable.

Section 5

Table Conventions

The rules are simple. The food moves to the center. The naan becomes equipment. The raita waits nearby like a tiny dairy fire department.

Share the dishes

Everything comes to the center. You will eat from multiple bowls. This is correct behavior.

Raita is your friend

Cool yogurt sauce. Dab it on anything that gets too spicy. Available as a side.

Naan is a utensil

Tear, scoop, eat. You may use a fork. No one will judge you to your face.

Emergency protocol: identify the raita bowl, apply a cooling dab, breathe normally, resume dinner.

Mango Lassi is a yogurt smoothie. It is dessert pretending to be a drink. Order one.

Prepared in a flat-line, 1970s airline-safety-card spirit. No actual emergency exits are located behind the chutney.
