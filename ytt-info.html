Yoga Ethics — Definitions

Ahimsa (अहिंसा)

Meaning: Non-harming in thought, word, and deed.

Includes subtle aggression, self-talk, tone, passive harm, and forcing the body.

Practice: speak truth without cruelty, set boundaries calmly, avoid internal self-attack.

Satya (सत्य)

Meaning: Truthfulness aligned with reality.

Integrity between inner knowing, spoken word, and action.

Practice: avoid exaggeration, admit uncertainty, notice where ego bends truth.

Satya without ahimsa becomes cruelty. Ahimsa without satya becomes avoidance.

Sangha (संघ)

Meaning: Spiritual community.

The field of shared discipline and mutual support.

Practice: show up consistently, support others, avoid divisiveness.

Abhyasa (अभ्यास)

Meaning: Sustained, repeated practice.

Consistency over intensity. Returning again and again.

Paired with non-attachment, abhyasa stabilizes the mind.
