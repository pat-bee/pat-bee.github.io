Meditation

Find a comfortable seat — maybe grab a blanket or bolster or block to sit on. Place hands down to be grounded, or place palms facing up to receive energy. Close the eyes, then look to the third eye. If thoughts are present, they are like clouds in a sky — look between the clouds and check in with how you're feeling.

Think of an intention for the class. Maybe stillness, maybe release, maybe strength. Set your intention and bring it into the class.

Deep Breathing

Open the eyes. We'll start with deep breathing — right hand on stomach, left hand on heart. Inhale through the nose, belly out. Exhale through nose or mouth, release. Inhale… exhale… inhale… and exhale. Continue on your own.

An invitation to be present in the class. Start by being present with each breath, and we'll link breath with movement.

Seated Warm-Up

Inhale, reach arms up overhead. Exhale, twist — right fingertips float down. Inhale to center. Exhale, left fingertips slow down. Inhale up. Exhale right. Inhale up and exhale left. Inhale up, exhale go to the right and stay — left hand on right knee, right hand back by the opposite hip. Spine lengthens, look back and breathe.

Inhale, reach up. Exhale, other side — hold and breathe. Inhale, reach up. Exhale, right fingertips down, left arm up and over. Look up. Inhale to center. Exhale, left fingertips down, right arm up and over. Look up. Inhale to center.

Exhale, go to the right and stay — maybe walk fingers out to deepen. Relax and breathe. Inhale, reach up. Exhale, other side — maybe walk fingers out, relax and breathe. Inhale, reach up.

Exhale, soften the hands and roll the shoulders — slow circles both directions. Imagine tension floating off.

Soften the shoulders. Tuck the chin to the chest — right ear to right shoulder, chin to chest, left ear to left shoulder. Chin to chest, back to the right, chin to chest, then to the left.

Bring chin to chest, go to the right and stay. Left fingertips down, right hand up — just the weight of the hand deepens the release, you don't need to pull. Left forearm level to the ground, move back and forth to loosen the neck. Then other side — chin to chest, head to the left, right fingertips down, left hand up, right forearm level to the ground, back and forth.

Tuck the chin to the chest, interlace fingers at the back of the neck, make slow circles with support. If it's comfortable, you can bring the hands down and let the head roll without support.

Seated Cat-Cow & Child's Pose

Seated cat-cow. Inhale, shoulders open, head back — cow. Exhale, curl down, look to the belly — cat. A few of these on your own.

Let's come to tabletop. If you'd like to support the knees with a blanket, bring that now. Hands under shoulders, knees under hips. Cat-cow here — inhale, U-shape in the spine, heart open. Exhale, curl up like an angry cat, gaze to the belly. Inhale cow, exhale cat.

On the next exhale, sink the hips to child's pose. You can bring knees out to the side to open hips a little more. Inhale back to cow, heart open. Exhale, child's pose. Continue on your own at a comfortable pace, gently warming up the body.

The next time you go to child's pose, stay there. Maybe rock the head side to side, grounding the mind and body. Walk the hands to the left for a side stretch, right hand on top of the left to deepen. Then other side — same thing.

Return to center. Walk the fingertips out a little more to lengthen the spine, then find downward dog — curl the toes, lift the hips up. If you have a blanket, bring that off the mat. Pedal the legs out, maybe shake the head yes and no. Take a few deep breaths, let the head and neck soften.

Mountain Pose & Sun Salutation A

Step to the top. Lengthen halfway up, fold forward, tuck the chin to the chest, slowly — one vertebra at a time — rise up. The head is the last thing to rise. Pause in mountain pose. Toes together, heels slightly open, shoulders a little back. Maybe close the eyes and take a few deep breaths.

This pose is called Mountain Pose, or Tadasana. It brings us to a quiet stillness. Samastitihi — take a few moments to rest in that peace.

We'll move to Sun Salutation A. Inhale, reach arms overhead. Exhale, fold forward. Inhale, torso halfway lifts. Exhale, right knee back, left meets it, torso toward elbows. Chaturanga. Push the arms straight — up dog. Engage the core and glutes, back to downward dog. Take a few deep breaths here, then back to the top.

Lengthen halfway up, fold forward, one vertebra at a time rise up. Repeat — reach the arms up, fold forward, halfway lift. Left knee back, right meets it, torso toward elbows. Chaturanga. Up dog, engage, back to down dog. Hold here — maybe heels closer to the ground. Breathe deep, then back to the top.

One more time — option to bring legs in to plank. Reach up, fold down, torso halfway lifts. Right leg straight back, left meets it. You can bring the knees down if you prefer. Chaturanga, up dog, down dog. Hold for a few breaths.

Back to the top, lengthen, release, rise up. Take that once more on your own — your favorite style. Link the breath with the movement at a pace that feels good to you.

Standing Poses

Meet back at the top. Lengthen halfway up, release down, slowly rise up. Reach up, interlace fingers, pointer fingers extend — reach to the right and look to the left. Back to center. Reach to the left, look to the right. Back to center.

Open the feet about hip distance apart, hands to hips. Forward fold — walk the hands down the fronts of the legs. Keep hands on shins, or bring peace fingers over the big toes and gently pull the body down. Pada Hastasana — a back release. Bend the knees as much as you'd like.

Hands to shins, lengthen halfway up, then take our rag doll — hands to either elbows, sway side to side. Tuck the chin to the chest, hands to hips, slowly rise up.

We'll reverse — bring hands to low back, shoulder blades toward each other, really slowly let the head fall back, lift up. Then take a vinyasa, or take child's pose — we'll all meet in downward dog.

Warrior Sequence — Right Side

From down dog, bring the right foot between the hands. Option one: back knee low for a low lunge. Option two: balance on the ball of the back foot for a high lunge. Look up, open arms like a cactus, shoulder blades toward one another. Interlace fingers at low back, heart opens, head back.

Find Warrior Two stance — back heel grounds, toes 45 degrees forward. Fingers interlaced for humble warrior, heart opens, fold just inside the front knee. Rise to Warrior Two — hips face the long edge of the mat, front knee goes back a touch to open the hips. Look to the front fingertips. Breathe deep.

Reverse — front palm flips up, back hand to back thigh, arch back. Side angle — front elbow to front thigh, left arm reaches up, torso slightly tilts up.

Bring both hands inside the front foot for lizard. Walk the right foot out to the side, gently move the hips back and forth. Option to lower the back knee. Maybe bring the right knee out to the side, right hand gently pushing that open. Or bring the elbows to a block or the floor. Listen to what feels good.

Grab props — blanket for the back knee, blocks to frame the front foot. Right foot forward, left knee down, back leg at an angle for a stretch in the front of the left hip. Flow: inhale heart opens, exhale hips back, inhale forward, exhale back. A few times.

Bring the right leg out to the side, blocks closer. Flow here — inhale forward, exhale hips back, a few rounds.

Turn to the long edge for gate pose — left knee down, right leg extended. Palms to prayer, then reach the right hand to the right shin, left arm up and over. Side stretch. Opposite side. A few rounds each way.

Rise up, frame the front leg, send hips back to stretch the right leg. Child's pose — knees out to either edge, arms forward, forehead toward the floor. Notice how the right side feels.

Warrior Sequence — Left Side

Lift up to downward facing dog. Left foot between the hands. Option one: right knee low for a low lunge. Option two: high lunge on the ball of the back foot. Gaze up, open arms like a cactus, interlace fingers at low back, heart opens, head back.

Find Warrior Two — back heel grounds, toes 45 degrees forward. Humble warrior, fingers interlaced, fold just inside the front knee. Rise to Warrior Two, arms level, look to front fingertips. Feel the openness in the hips.

Reverse — front palm flips up, arch back. Side angle — front elbow to front thigh, top arm reaches up. Both hands inside the front foot for lizard, walk the left foot out. Explore what feels good on this side.

Grab props — blanket for right knee, blocks frame the left foot. Flow: inhale heart open, exhale hips back. A few rounds. Bring left leg out to the side. Flow: inhale forward, exhale hips back. A few rounds.

Turn to the side for gate pose — right knee down, left leg extended. Palms to prayer, left hand to left shin, right arm up and over. Side stretch both ways, a few rounds.

Rise up, hips sink back to stretch the left leg. Child's pose — knees out to the side. Option to wrap the hands back and grip the outsides of the feet to release the shoulders differently. Breathe deeply and notice how you feel after working both sides.

Wide-Legged Fold & Mountain

Find down dog, curl the toes, lift up. Left foot forward, turn to face the long edge of the mat. Toes slightly in, heels slightly out. Arms level to the ground — pause here, energize through the fingertips and up through the top of the head.

Hands to hips, lengthen up, fold forward. Hands to the ground or on blocks. Let the low back release.

Tuck the chin, hands to hips, slowly rise. One more time — reach the arms out, interlace fingers at low back, heart opens, fall forward. Let gravity work. Tuck the chin, slowly rise up.

Step to the top of the mat. Mountain pose — toes together, heels slightly open, shoulders a bit back. Maybe close the eyes. Breathe deeply. Samastitihi. Ground the feet. Imagine roots growing through the feet, sinking deep into the ground.

Tree Pose & Chair Pose

Keep that strength going into tree pose. Left foot is strong. Bring the right foot — toes might stay on the ground with heel at the inner calf, or maybe the whole foot to the inner calf, inner thigh, or a half lotus near the hip crease. This is a hip opener. Bring the knee back a little to open more. Palms to prayer, maybe reach up. Breathe deep. Slowly release with intention.

Right foot hip distance from the left. Sink to chair — reach the arms up, shoulders stay soft. Rise up, find mountain again. Roots sinking down to the earth. A few deep breaths.

Tree pose on the other side — right foot strong. Left toes might stay low with heel at inner calf, whole foot to inner calf or inner thigh, or deeper with left knee back. Palms to prayer, maybe rise up. Slowly release. Left foot hip distance from the right. Sink to chair, shoulders soft. Look up, then rise up.

Yogi Squat & Cobbler's Pose

Take a yogi squat — blocks stacked make a little throne to sit on. Heels and toes out, palms to prayer. Focus on pushing elbows against the knees to open the hips. A few deep breaths — close the eyes and remember your intentions.

Find your way to seated and remove the blocks. Cobbler's pose — soles of the feet together, hands open the feet like a book, elbows gently press down. Notice the opening of the hips.

Frog Pose

Rise up. For frog pose, turn to the long edge and bring the knees out. The hips are in the same line as the knees — a bit different from child's pose. You might stagger with your neighbor. Bring a blanket for the elbows to rest on, or use blocks for the head or arms. This is a really deep pose — we've done excellent work warming up for this. Stay here for a few moments.

As you inhale, imagine light moving up the spine through the top of the head. As you exhale, imagine smoke releasing from the base of the spine. This is an expulsion meditation — each breath brings in fresh energy and releases what no longer serves us.

Rise up. Take a child's pose right where you are — knees out, just sink the hips back. Forehead can rest on a block or blanket. Let the hips release.

Shoulder Work

Hips rise up. Find tabletop — hands under shoulders, knees under hips. Thread the needle: left hand straight out, reach the right arm up, then weave it beneath the left. A few deep breaths — let the body soften, let gravity work. Keep the left hand steady. Reach the right arm back up to release.

Other side — right arm stretches out, left hand reaches up, weave the arm beneath. Notice any variation from side to side. Keep the right hand steady. Left hand reaches up to release.

Puppy dog pose — hips stay over the knees, reach both arms out, chin or forehead lowers to the ground. Nice bend in the back and release in the shoulders.

Variation of child's pose using blocks — knees out to the side, or knees together if the low back needs support. Reach the hands out onto the blocks and let the body soften. This deeply stretches the trapezoids and shoulders.

Bridge & Restorative Poses

Rise up. Swing the feet off to one side and settle into bridge pose — reclined, arms stretched out, raise the heels. Flow first: inhale, lift the hips up; exhale, lower hips down. Inhale, lift — arms might rise up overhead. Exhale, everything down. Continue a few times on your own.

Next time the hips come low, stay low. Restorative bridge — lift the body up and grab a block at the medium setting, just above the seat. Hands rest down. For more depth, bring the block to the taller setting. This works the key areas from today: hips, low back, and shoulders. Bridge is also a gentle inversion.

Remove the block. Happy baby — feet toward the ceiling, hands grab the outsides of the feet, gently rock side to side. Maybe kick one leg straight.

Spine twist — reach the arms out to either side, legs fall to the right, look to the left. Gazing away from the knees opens up the neck. Then slowly to the other side.

Come back to center. Reclined pigeon — left foot grounded, right foot crosses on the left in a figure four. Option to interlace fingers beneath the left thigh and gently pull in. Then take it to the other side. Release, hug both knees to the chest and gently rock side to side — anything that feels good.

Restorative Fish

Roll off to one side in the fetal position, then gently push up. We'll take a gentle restorative fish. Fold the blanket — to keep this really gentle, fold it once and place it vertical behind you. To make it more intense, fold it one more time so it's like a mini bolster, and leave some space for your head at the top.

Sit right in front of the blanket. If you'd like to support the hips, bring blocks to open them — soles of the feet together. Lie back, the heart opens. You can stretch the legs out if you prefer. I'll offer shoulder adjustments with lavender essential oil — if you don't want this, please place one hand on the stomach.

If you are happy in this pose, you can stay here as we move into the sound bath, or settle into a more traditional savasana. In traditional savasana, legs are extended out. Props under the knees help the low back and hips soften. You can also try a block or two on the belly for the feeling of being weighted down, which deepens relaxation.

Coming Back

Bring awareness to the physical body. Wiggle toes and fingers. Maybe stretch the arms up like you are just waking up in the morning. Bring knees toward the chest, remove any blocks from the belly, then rock side to side.

Legs fall to your favorite side — use the arm on that side like a pillow. Moving from corpse pose, which symbolizes death, to the fetal pose, symbolizing new life. When you feel ready, rise to seated.

Closing

Remember whatever intentions you started with. You've cultivated those intentions during our class — bring them to the world.
